## Context
This document provides detailed case-by-case documentation of the first 10 pneumonia cases (1974-1979) experienced by Peter "Pneumonia Pete" Sebastian during his early childhood years, establishing the initial pattern of his unique medical history.

## Early Childhood Cases (1974-1979)

### Philip (#1, 1974) - "The Original"
- Age: 4 years
- Duration: 67 days
- Notable: First clinical death (2:17); established personification pattern
- Medical significance: Initial discovery of unusual recovery capacity
- Named after pediatric doctor who pronounced him "medically fascinating"

### Andrew (#2, 1975) - "The Awakener"
- Age: 5 years
- Duration: 54 days
- Notable: First instance of maintaining consciousness during severe symptoms
- Medical significance: Unusual cognitive function during respiratory distress
- Led to first specialized medical documentation

### Barbara (#3, 1975) - "The Persistent"
- Age: 5 years
- Duration: 38 days
- Notable: Occurred immediately after recovery from Case #2
- Medical significance: First evidence of adaptive response pattern
- Named for nurse who documented symptom variations

### Charles (#4, 1976) - "The Teacher"
- Age: 6 years
- Duration: 42 days
- Notable: First documented case of Pete self-managing symptoms
- Medical significance: Development of patient-directed treatment modifications
- Named for elementary school teacher who provided home instruction

### Diana (#5, 1976) - "The Quiet One"
- Age: 6 years
- Duration: 29 days
- Notable: First instance of minimal external symptoms despite severe internal condition
- Medical significance: Beginning of "invisible pneumonia" pattern
- Named for respiratory therapist who detected hidden symptoms

### Edward (#6, 1977) - "The Rival"
- Age: 7 years
- Duration: 51 days
- Notable: Second clinical death (3:42); combative recovery pattern
- Medical significance: First documented immune overreaction
- Named for hospital roommate who recovered more slowly

### Frederick (#7, 1977) - "The Chameleon"
- Age: 7 years
- Duration: 33 days
- Notable: Unusual symptom presentation mimicking other conditions
- Medical significance: First documentation of atypical pneumonia presentation
- Named for diagnostic physician who identified true condition

### Victoria (#8, 1978) - "The Companion"
- Age: 8 years
- Duration: 47 days
- Notable: First case where Pete maintained full consciousness throughout
- Medical significance: Established cognitive functioning protocol during respiratory distress
- Named for grandmother who stayed throughout hospitalization

### George (#9, 1978) - "The Gentleman"
- Age: 8 years
- Duration: 26 days
- Notable: First case with minimal hospitalization requirement
- Medical significance: Beginning of home treatment protocol development
- Named for family doctor who approved home care plan

### Harriet (#10, 1979) - "The Harmonizer"
- Age: 9 years
- Duration: 31 days
- Notable: First synchronized treatment with multiple specialists
- Medical significance: Development of integrated care approach
- Named for care coordinator who created treatment integration

## Early Pattern Summary

### Initial Case Progression
- First hospitalization (#1): Severe crisis with unexpected survival
- Rapid recurrence pattern (#2-3): Limited recovery time between cases
- Self-management emergence (#4-5): Development of patient-directed approach
- Fluctuating severity (#6-7): Unpredictable intensity between cases
- Adaptation evolution (#8-10): Progressive development of coping mechanisms

### Medical Response Evolution
- Initial diagnostic confusion (#1-3): Limited understanding of condition
- Treatment experimentation (#4-6): Various approaches with inconsistent results
- Collaborative framework (#7-10): Integration of patient feedback into protocols
- Documentation enhancement: Progressive improvement in clinical records
- Research interest: Beginning of formal study of Pete's unique condition
