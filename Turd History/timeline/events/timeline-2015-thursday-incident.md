## Context
This document details the infamous "Thursday Incident" of 2015, a pivotal confrontation between Frederick "Fred" Turd and Lawrence "Larry" Francis Bird during a Turd Bird Industries Board meeting. This watershed event transformed their professional antagonism into open conflict, led to Fred's temporary removal as CEO, and fundamentally altered the company's power dynamics and operational approach.

## Event Timeline

### Pre-Meeting Tensions (Early 2015)

#### ReAnimus Initiative Development
- Fred's secretive advancement of reanimation technology research
- Gradual expansion beyond initially approved parameters
- Recruitment of Dr. Dennis "D.C." Ease to lead experimental protocols
- Implementation of increasingly questionable research methods
- Development of first successful reanimation protocols
- Establishment of dedicated underground research facility
- Beginning of human subject experimentation without proper oversight

#### Larry's Growing Concerns
- Initial discovery of full scope of reanimation research
- Identification of ethical violations and regulatory risks
- Documentation of safety protocol inadequacies
- Growing recognition of existential risks to company
- Private meeting with Fred attempting to establish boundaries
- Compilation of comprehensive risk assessment documentation
- Strategic preparation for Board intervention

### The Board Meeting (Thursday, June 18, 2015)

#### Meeting Setup
- Quarterly strategic review Board meeting in secure conference room
- Full Board attendance including all members
- Unique presence of The Board Director in medically-induced coma
- Recording systems secretly disabled by Fred prior to meeting
- Pneumonia Pete present as operations representative
- Tense atmosphere following weeks of private disagreement
- Unusual security protocols implemented by Fred's team

#### Presentation Sequence
1. Fred presents misleadingly positive ReAnimus Initiative progress update
   - Selective data highlighting successes while omitting failures
   - Minimization of containment breaches and research subject issues
   - Emphasis on commercial applications without risk disclosure
   - Presentation of manipulated safety statistics 
   - Strategic framing of research as revolutionary scientific breakthrough
   - Suppression of key ethical concerns raised by research staff

2. Larry challenges accuracy of presented information
   - Presentation of contradictory internal documentation
   - Revelation of concealed containment failures
   - Disclosure of unauthorized protocol modifications
   - Evidence of regulatory violations and legal exposure
   - Documentation of misleading Board communications
   - Presentation of comprehensive risk assessment data

#### Confrontation Escalation
1. Fred dismisses concerns as "bureaucratic handwringing"
2. Larry presents evidence of deliberate Board deception
3. Fred questions Larry's loyalty to company vision
4. Larry reveals documentation of ethical violations
5. Fred becomes increasingly agitated during exchange
6. Heated argument escalates with personal accusations
7. Larry presents evidence of potential legal liability
8. Fred's demeanor shifts from defensive to aggressive

#### Critical Moment: The "Francis Underpantsis" Deployment
- Fred deliberately shifts from professional to personal attack
- Strategic deployment of childhood trigger during formal presentation
- Public use of "Francis Underpantsis" nickname in front of full Board
- Calculated to undermine Larry's professional credibility
- Designed to distract from substantive ReAnimus concerns
- Deliberately targeting known emotional vulnerability
- Unprecedented breach of professional boundaries in corporate setting

#### Larry's Response and Board Reaction
- Brief but visible emotional reaction from Larry (tears noted)
- Remarkably composed recovery surprising observers
- Maintenance of professional focus despite personal attack
- Board's evident shock at Fred's breach of decorum
- Pneumonia Pete's documentation of Board members' reactions
- Meeting temporarily adjourned for "technical issues"
- Board Director in medically-induced coma registering unexpected vital signs

- Extraordinary session called without Fred and Larry present
- Decision to implement immediate cooling-off period
